Nutrition & Grooming
Food
It’s important to feed the correct food that matches the dogs age (Puppy, Adult or Senior). For the proper amount of food you can find the suggestions on the bag itself but 2 to 3 times a day is the ideal food intake for Frenchies. A lot of obesity issues arise in French Bulldogs due to the “Short Muzzle” I mentioned earlier. Keeping a dog in healthy weight and shape will lower the chances of them having any back, spine or hip issues. Make sure when you give them treats to do so in moderation, and give them treats that match their exercise levels, to prevent unhealthy weight gain.
Water
Frenchies need access to plenty of water, especially on hotter days than usual. Due to brachycephalic muzzles they can overheat easily. It would be very helpful to invest in a portable water source for Frenchie owners when you decide to go and take them out for a walk or just have some water accessible for them when they are outside exercising.
Grooming
Brushing weekly will do the trick as they shed constantly but it’s minimal hair that they shed. Brushing encourages new hair growth which then in return distributes hair oils throughout their skin keeping them looking healthy. Also ensure to clean out their lip folds and to be careful washing their faces and not get water to run into their eyes or ears. Lastly ensure to trim their nails so that long nails won’t cause them pain, you can do this by trimming them yourself or if you walk your dog on pavement you can naturally let the pavement trim their nails.
